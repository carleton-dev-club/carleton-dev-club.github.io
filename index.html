About Carleton .DevClub()

The Carleton dev club is a group dedicated to helping students to develop their coding skills, while pushing them to develop a portfolio, and to network with their peers.

The Carleton dev club meets every Tuesday, from 5:30 to 8:30 in HP4125. We will be hosting minor events, tech talks, and workshops periodically for students to learn new and exciting languages, and ways of applying their knowledge.

Volunteering With Us

As a student run group, we would look for volunteers who are willing to help us with our events. As a dev club volunteer, you will be helping students with problems they may have, offering guidance and ideas, as well as promoting a positive and collaborative environment for all our attendees. We are also going to offer CCR credits for interested, and outstanding individuals who show that they understand the spirit of the carleton dev club.

If you think you would be interested in volunteering with us, please fill out this form, and we will reach out to you with information regarding your role.

Our Code Of Conduct

The Carleton dev club aims to provide an instructive, and positive workspace for computer science students to be able to work, and grow their skillsets, all the while helping each other to learn new things. As such, we require that all attendees are on their best behaviour, and not try to put others down.

We hope for all our attendees to be able to feel that coming to this area will be an exciting and helpful experience, and as such we cannot tolerate offensive, or rude language, or actions. Should you be found to be causing any problems, you will be dealt with by the dev club executives. Should you feel that someone is attacking you, or making you feel unwelcome, please inform us, and we will deal with it immediately.
